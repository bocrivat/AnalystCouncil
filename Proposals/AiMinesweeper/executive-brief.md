# Executive Brief: AI Minesweeper

**Positioning**: The first Minesweeper with a built-in logic coach that teaches you *why* — not just where the mines are.

## The Opportunity
Minesweeper is experiencing a genuine cultural revival (Netflix launch, viral social media, 10M+ players on minesweeper.online), yet no PC/Steam game offers coaching that explains the logic behind moves. The niche is small but real: comparable games show 50K–200K potential lifetime buyers. At $6.99 with realistic execution, this is a **$35K–$70K net year-one opportunity** (base scenario) — a viable indie project, not a venture play.

## Key Insight
Minesweeper is the most-played game that most people don't actually understand. Millions believe it's a guessing game. A coach that explains the *why* behind every move — transforming frustration into mastery — is a product category that doesn't exist on any PC platform. This is a genuine head start, not a permanent moat — competitors can replicate in months, so speed and community depth are everything.

## AI Sentiment: The Good News
The headline stats look hostile: 84% of PC users won't pay extra for AI features, 85% of gamers are below-neutral on GenAI. But Quantic Foundry's granular data shows **coaching/tutorial AI is the least-hated AI use case** — far less toxic than AI art (83% negative) or narrative generation (77% negative). Our AI coach is functionally a personalized tutorial, sitting in the most favorable corner of an otherwise hostile landscape. We should still brand as "smart coach" over "AI-powered," but the concept itself has more consumer permission than the headlines suggest.
